## ‚öñÔ∏è Operational Grey Areas
<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="5. The 'Vaporware' Sales" icon="ghost" iconType="solid" color="#34495E" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/34495E/ffffff?text=Fake+It" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** A Fortune 500 wants to buy a feature you haven't built yet. Sales says "Yes, we have it." Engineering says "It will take 6 months."
    * **Decision:** Sign the contract and crunch, or be honest and lose the deal?
  </Card>
  <Card title="6. Competitor Lawsuit" icon="scale-balanced" iconType="solid" color="#2980B9" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/2980B9/ffffff?text=IP+Lawsuit" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** A massive competitor sues you for patent infringement (even though you didn't copy). Legal fees will bankrupt you.
    * **Decision:** Settle (and pay royalties forever) or fight (and likely die)?
  </Card>
  <Card title="7. The Toxic Rainmaker" icon="biohazard" iconType="solid" color="#16A085" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/16A085/ffffff?text=Culture+Vs+Rev" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** Your VP Sales brings in 80% of revenue but harasses junior staff. HR has complaints. Firing them misses the quarter targets.
    * **Decision:** Revenue vs. Culture.
  </Card>
  <Card title="8. Data Privacy Breach" icon="user-secret" iconType="solid" color="#F1C40F" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/F1C40F/ffffff?text=GDPR+Leak" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** You accidentally leak customer emails. It's minor. Reporting it triggers a PR nightmare. Fixing it quietly is technically illegal.
    * **Decision:** Ethics vs. Optics.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## ‚ôüÔ∏è Strategic Dilemmas
<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="9. The 'Whale' Trap" icon="building" iconType="solid" color="#2C3E50" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/2C3E50/ffffff?text=Enterprise+Client" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** A huge client offers a $1M contract, but demands you build custom features that don't fit your roadmap.
    * **Decision:** Become a "Consultancy" for cash, or stay a "Product" company and starve?
  </Card>
  <Card title="10. The Pricing Revolt" icon="tag" iconType="solid" color="#D35400" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/D35400/ffffff?text=Price+Hike" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** You need to double your prices to be profitable. Your loyal early users (who pay little) threaten to leave and leave bad reviews.
    * **Decision:** Grandfather them in (lose money) or force the upgrade (bad PR)?
  </Card>
  <Card title="11. Speed vs. Quality" icon="bug" iconType="solid" color="#8E44AD" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/8E44AD/ffffff?text=Tech+Debt" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** Competitor is launching next week. Your product is buggy. Do you launch now to be "First," or wait 2 months to be "Better"?
    * **Decision:** First Mover Advantage vs. Reputation Risk.
  </Card>
  <Card title="12. Platform Dependency" icon="link-slash" iconType="solid" color="#C0392B" img="https://placehold.co/600x400/C0392B/ffffff?text=API+Risk" cta="Analyze">
    **Scenario:** You built your business on OpenAI's API. OpenAI releases a feature that does exactly what you do for free.
    * **Decision:** Pivot immediately or try to compete on UX?
  </Card>
</Columns>

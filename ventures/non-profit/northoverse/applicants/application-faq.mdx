## üìù General Application Process

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="How do I apply for a program?" icon="pen-to-square" iconType="solid">
    **Applications are submitted online** through our portal. Each program (ETP, Women Founders, etc.) has its own specific application form. Simply navigate to the program page and click "Apply" to begin the intake questionnaire.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Can I apply to more than one track?" icon="layer-group" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** However, we recommend focusing on **one primary track at a time** to get the most value. For example, you can complete the **ETP (Training Program)** first to validate your idea, and then apply for the **Business Plan Assistance** track once your concept is solid.
  </Card>

  <Card title="What happens after I submit my application?" icon="hourglass-start" iconType="solid">
    Our specialized selection committee reviews applications on a rolling basis. You will typically receive an email confirmation immediately, followed by a status update or interview request within **5‚Äì10 business days**.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Can I re-apply if I am not accepted?" icon="rotate-right" iconType="solid">
    **Absolutely.** We encourage persistence. If you are not accepted, we will provide feedback on why (e.g., the idea needs more research, or the cohort was full). You are welcome to improve your application and try again for the next cycle.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Do I need a full team to apply?" icon="users" iconType="solid">
    **No.** Solo founders are very welcome, especially in the **Entrepreneurial Training Program (ETP)**. However, for the **Women Founders Circle**, having a core team in place can be beneficial for handling the workload.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Is there an age limit?" icon="id-card" iconType="solid">
    **Generally, no.** We accept applicants from High School age (16+) to retirees. However, applicants under 18 may require a parent/guardian consent form for legal liability reasons during events.
  </Card>

</Columns>

## üìã Eligibility & Requirements

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="Do I need to be incorporated?" icon="building" iconType="solid">
    **No.** Most of our programs (ETP, Indigenous Pathway, Business Plan Assistance) are designed to help you *get* incorporated. We guide you through that process. Only the **Women Founders Circle** generally prefers businesses that are already established entities.
  </Card>

  <Card title="I am an Indigenous founder, how do I verify eligibility?" icon="feather" iconType="solid">
    We operate on a model of trust and inclusion. While we may ask for self-identification regarding First Nations, M√©tis, or Inuit heritage to ensure the **Indigenous Pathway** resources reach the right people, we generally do not require strict federal status cards for initial entry.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Does my business idea need to be 'Tech'?" icon="microchip" iconType="solid">
    **No.** Northoverse is industry-agnostic. We welcome social enterprises, retail concepts, service businesses, arts organizations, and tech startups. If it's a business, we can help you build it.
  </Card>

  <Card title="I am an international student, can I join?" icon="passport" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** If you are currently residing in the region on a valid student visa, you are eligible for our programs. However, please note that we cannot provide legal immigration advice regarding starting a business while on a visa.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Do I need a finished business plan?" icon="file-lines" iconType="solid">
    **Not for ETP or Indigenous Pathway.** Those programs help you write one. However, for **Business Plan Assistance**, you should have at least a rough draft or raw data ready so our strategists can help you refine it.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Is the Women Founders Circle inclusive of non-binary founders?" icon="venus" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** The program is a safe space designed to support anyone who identifies as a woman or non-binary individual navigating the specific challenges of the business world.
  </Card>

</Columns>

## üí∞ Costs, Funding & Equity

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="Is there a cost to join?" icon="dollar-sign" iconType="solid">
    **Most programs are fully subsidized** by our partners and grants.
    * **Digital Classrooms:** 100% Free.
    * **Indigenous & Women's Tracks:** Full scholarships are often available. In some cases, a small **commitment fee** (e.g., $50) is charged to ensure attendance, which is **fully refunded** upon successful completion of the program.
    * **ETP:** May have a small refundable commitment fee.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Does Northoverse take equity (ownership) of my company?" icon="chart-pie" iconType="solid">
    **No.** We are an NGO incubator. We do not take equity, shares, or ownership in your company in exchange for our standard mentorship and programming. Your business remains 100% yours.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Do you provide grant funding directly?" icon="hand-holding-dollar" iconType="solid">
    **Generally, no.** Northoverse provides the *training* to help you apply for grants. We do not usually give cash directly to founders. However, we occasionally host "Pitch Competitions" where small cash prizes are awarded.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Are the refundable fees guaranteed?" icon="rotate-left" iconType="solid">
    **Yes,** provided you meet the attendance requirements (usually 80% attendance). If you drop out without notice, the commitment fee is donated to the Northoverse student scholarship fund.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Who pays for the Digital Classrooms?" icon="school" iconType="solid">
    **Nobody.** Thanks to our corporate sponsors and government grants, all digital curriculum modules for high schools and colleges are provided completely free of charge.
  </Card>

</Columns>

## üîí Intellectual Property (IP) & Privacy

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="Is my business idea safe with you?" icon="shield-halved" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** All Northoverse staff, mentors, and program managers sign strict confidentiality agreements. We treat your application data with the highest level of privacy.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Do I need to sign an NDA?" icon="signature" iconType="solid">
    **We do not sign NDAs** at the application stage. This is industry standard (investors and incubators see thousands of ideas). However, once you are accepted into a cohort, we establish clear rules of engagement regarding privacy among cohort members.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Who owns the IP created during the program?" icon="copyright" iconType="solid">
    **You do.** Any product, code, business plan, or brand asset you create while in a Northoverse program belongs solely to you. Northoverse claims no ownership over your intellectual property.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Will my idea be shared publicly?" icon="bullhorn" iconType="solid">
    Only if you want it to be. We often showcase startup successes on our social media, but we will **always** ask for your permission before posting anything about your specific business.
  </Card>

</Columns>

## üéì For Educators (Digital Classrooms)

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="Do schools need to apply to use the curriculum?" icon="chalkboard-user" iconType="solid">
    **No formal application is needed.** The Digital Classrooms are "Open Access." Educators can register simply to track downloads, but there is no approval process. You can download and deploy immediately.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Can I modify the curriculum?" icon="pencil" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** Our materials are provided under a Creative Commons license. You are free to adapt, shorten, or expand the slide decks and assignments to fit your specific classroom needs.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Does Northoverse provide teachers?" icon="person-chalkboard" iconType="solid">
    **No.** These are "Teacher-Led" resources. We provide the slides, notes, and assignments, but the classroom teacher is responsible for delivering the content.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Can these modules be used for Homeschooling?" icon="house-laptop" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** The modules are designed to be self-contained and are excellent for homeschool parents looking to add a business or entrepreneurship elective to their curriculum.
  </Card>

</Columns>

## üìç Logistics & Commitment

<Columns cols={1}>

  <Card title="Are the programs in-person or remote?" icon="wifi" iconType="solid">
    **It is Hybrid.**
    * **ETP:** Usually requires in-person attendance on weekends at the Northoverse Hub.
    * **Indigenous & Women's Tracks:** Often feature a mix of virtual workshops and in-person community circles.
    * **Business Plan Assistance:** Can be done 100% remotely via Zoom.
  </Card>

  <Card title="What is the time commitment?" icon="clock" iconType="solid">
    * **ETP:** 4 Weekends (approx. 6 hours per weekend).
    * **Women Founders Circle:** 2 hours per week for 8 weeks.
    * **Indigenous Pathway:** Flexible/Self-paced with monthly check-ins.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Where is the Northoverse Hub located?" icon="map-pin" iconType="solid">
    Our physical headquarters and coworking space are located in **[City, Province]**. We also have partner spaces in select universities for student outreach events.
  </Card>

  <Card title="What if I have to miss a session?" icon="calendar-xmark" iconType="solid">
    We understand life happens. If you miss one session, we usually provide recordings or notes. However, missing more than 20% of the sessions may result in forfeiting your "Certificate of Completion" or your refundable commitment fee.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Do I get a certificate?" icon="certificate" iconType="solid">
    **Yes.** Upon successful completion of the Entrepreneurial Training Program (ETP) or the Women Founders Circle, participants receive a digital certificate from Northoverse, which can be added to LinkedIn or resumes.
  </Card>
